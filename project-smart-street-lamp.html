Motion Detecting Street Lamp & Gas Detecting Smart System

A smart street-lighting and safety system combining motion-based LED control with smoke / gas detection for safer and more energy-efficient urban lighting.

Overview

This project addressed two important concerns in urban environments: energy consumption and safety. By using motion detection, the system can dim or brighten LED streetlamps based on real activity, while gas/smoke detection adds an extra layer of environmental monitoring.

My Work & Responsibilities

  • Designed the circuit logic for motion-based LED control using Timer 555 IC and microcontroller logic.
  • Integrated gas / smoke sensors to detect hazardous conditions.
  • Used Proteus, Multisim, and TinkerCAD to simulate and validate the behaviour before building.
  • Implemented the system using Arduino Uno for control and prototyping.

Tools, Platforms & Components

  • Timer 555 IC for timing and pulse control applications.
  • Arduino Uno for microcontroller-based decision-making.
  • Proteus, TinkerCAD, and Multisim for circuit simulation and testing.
  • LEDs, motion sensors, and gas/smoke sensors as core hardware modules.

Why It Matters & What I Learned

This project helped me see how relatively simple components can be combined into practical smart-city solutions. I gained hands-on experience in sensor integration, timing circuits, and microcontroller programming. Most importantly, it reinforced the link between energy efficiency, safety, and responsible engineering design.
