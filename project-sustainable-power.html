Optimization Techniques for Sustainable Resource Management

A modeling-focused project where I used optimization techniques to design more efficient and sustainable resource allocation in engineering systems.

Overview

In this project, I explored how optimization can support sustainable engineering decisions. The idea was to move beyond intuitive or trial-and-error approaches and instead use mathematical models to balance efficiency, cost, and environmental impact. The system can represent anything from energy distribution to water or material usage.

My Role & What I Did

  • Defined the decision variables, constraints, and objective functions for a resource allocation problem.
  • Formulated optimization models to minimize environmental impact while maintaining performance targets.
  • Ran scenario analyses to see how different constraints (e.g., emissions limits, budget limits) affected results.
  • Interpreted the output and translated it into clear engineering recommendations.

Tools & Methods

  • Linear / nonlinear optimization concepts from operations research.
  • Spreadsheet or scripting tools (e.g., Python / Excel Solver–style tools) for solving optimization models.
  • Visualization of trade-offs between performance and sustainability metrics.

Impact & Learnings

This project showed me that sustainability is not just a qualitative goal—it can be expressed, optimized, and justified with data. I learned how important it is to clearly define objectives and constraints before jumping into solutions. It strengthened my ability to think in terms of trade-offs and helped me appreciate how optimization tools can support ethical, long-term engineering decisions.
