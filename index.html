What I Learned

ENGG 687 showed me that engineering ethics is not just about knowing the rules, but about understanding the real impact of decisions on people, communities, and the environment. I learned how factors like time pressure, workplace culture, and communication gaps can influence what engineers do in practice. Responsibility exists at many levels: individual, team, organization, and society.

My Biggest Takeaway

Click to flip and see what stayed with me.

My Biggest Takeaway

My biggest takeaway is that speaking up early matters. Many failures in the case studies did not come from a single bad decision, but from small concerns that were ignored or left unspoken. Moral courage, clear documentation, and honest communication are just as important as technical skills. As an engineer, staying silent can be as harmful as making a wrong calculation.

How I Will Integrate This

Click to flip and see my plan.

How I Will Integrate This

Going forward, I will integrate these lessons by consciously questioning assumptions, asking for clarification when something feels uncertain, and prioritizing public safety over convenience or speed. I want to contribute to a culture where teammates feel safe raising concerns. In my projects and future roles, I will design with long-term impacts in mind, document my reasoning clearly, and choose transparency even when it is uncomfortable.

ENGG 687 Portfolio

Key reflections and learnings from ENGG 687 – Ethics, Law, and the Engineering Profession.

Engineering Ethics & Responsibility

Ethical engineering is more than following rules; it means protecting people, society, and the environment. I learned how real decisions happen in messy, ambiguous situations, where honesty and moral courage really matter.

Course Learnings

Case studies like Challenger and Walkerton showed how system-level issues, communication gaps, and culture can lead to failure. I now see engineering as balancing technical innovation with long-term responsibility.

Reading Reflections

Readings on moral courage and unintended consequences helped me reflect on when to speak up, how designs affect real people, and why context matters just as much as calculations.

Suggested Materials

Additional resources on professional responsibility and sustainability reinforced that transparency, accountability, and environmental awareness are core parts of being an engineer.

Teamwork Reflection

Our project experience showed that clear roles, communication, and listening to each other are essential. Once we organized ourselves, our teamwork and outcomes improved a lot.

Looking Back & My Choice

Early in the course, ethics felt overwhelming. Now I see it as a skill I can grow. I chose resilience as my key attribute—staying focused under pressure, adapting, and learning from every challenge.
